About Me

I was originally interested in becoming a math professor. While pursuing my PhD, I found that this wasn't the right path for me and decided to make a change. I wanted to earn a decent living and have more time to pursue personal projects. I became more interested in things like artificial intelligence and wanted to apply my math background to developing new tools. I had already had a good background in programming (I was one of those Linux/C++ computer science purists who converted to math because I found it more fun), so I found myself in an ideal place to understand the theory and the practice behind these tools. My focus was primarily on differentiable dynamical systems, algebraic topology, and analysis, but I constantly felt inspired to phrase AI stuff in these languages. My eventual goal is to becomee a Machine Learning Engineer so that I could continue learning about the latest developments and glean inspiration for research projects.

I typically use Python, Tensorflow, Pytorch, SQL, and Sage in my research. I dabble in HTML/CSS/PHP, javascript, and AWS as well. I wrote this website and stood it up on Amazon.
